# How to prepare before asking for a website quote

If you send only the message 'how much does a website cost?', you will receive answers that are hard to compare. One provider may estimate a simple page, another a full website, another may include copy, SEO, design and maintenance.

A good quote starts with a good brief. It does not have to be perfect, but it should give enough context for the provider to understand what you want to achieve and what needs to be built.

## Why this matters in practice

A website can mean many things: presentation site, online store, booking platform, catalogue, landing page or a system connected to other tools. Without details, the price is only a guess.

## Questions the page needs to answer

A visitor does not read a website like a brochure. Most people arrive with a few practical questions in mind:

- What is the main objective of the website?
- Who is the audience and what should they do on the site?
- Which pages are needed?
- Which features are mandatory?
- Do you already have copy, photos, logo, domain and hosting?

## What the website should include

A good website does not need to be overloaded. It needs to be complete in the places that matter:

- Short description of the business and market.
- Objective: leads, sales, bookings, credibility, recruitment, information.
- List of desired pages and examples of websites close to the desired direction.
- Features: forms, payments, bookings, accounts, multilingual, blog, integrations.
- Available materials and materials that need to be created.

## Common mistakes

Most problems do not come from a lack of design. They come from unclear information. These are the mistakes worth avoiding:

- Comparing quotes only by price, without deliverables.
- Not saying what failed on the old site.
- Leaving important features until after signing.
- Not clarifying who writes the copy.
- Not asking about maintenance, support and ownership.

## What to prepare before starting

Before asking for a quote or starting a redesign, prepare a few basic things:

- Main objective in one sentence.
- List of pages and features.
- Existing materials: logo, photos, copy, presentations.
- Indicative budget and desired timeline.
- Available access: domain, hosting, Analytics, Search Console.

## How to know whether it works

A website should not be judged only by how it looks on launch day. Track whether people reach the important pages, understand the offer and send clear enquiries.

A good quote should be comparable: what is included, what is not, how many revision rounds exist, who provides content, what happens after launch and which recurring costs apply.

## Conclusion

The clearer the brief, the more realistic the quote. You do not need a complicated document, only concrete answers to the essential questions.
