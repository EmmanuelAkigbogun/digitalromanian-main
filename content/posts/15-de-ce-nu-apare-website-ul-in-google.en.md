# Why your website does not appear in Google and what to check first

When a website does not appear in Google, frustration is normal. Still, the problem is not always serious. Sometimes the page is not indexed, sometimes it is blocked technically, and sometimes it simply does not have enough relevant content.

The important thing is not to guess. Checks should be done in order, from simple technical issues to page quality and competition for the targeted searches.

## Why this matters in practice

Google does not automatically show every new page and does not guarantee good positions just because the website exists. To appear, the site must be accessible, understandable, indexable and useful for a search.

## Questions the page needs to answer

A visitor does not read a website like a brochure. Most people arrive with a few practical questions in mind:

- Is the website indexed?
- Has a sitemap been submitted in Search Console?
- Do important pages have noindex by mistake?
- Is the content clear enough for the topic?
- Are there speed, mobile or technical errors?

## What the website should include

A good website does not need to be overloaded. It needs to be complete in the places that matter:

- Google Search Console configured correctly.
- Updated XML sitemap submitted.
- Robots.txt file checked.
- Pages with titles, descriptions and unique content.
- Internal links between important pages.

## Common mistakes

Most problems do not come from a lack of design. They come from unclear information. These are the mistakes worth avoiding:

- Searching only the company name and drawing conclusions about all SEO.
- Assuming a plugin solves indexing.
- Copying content from other websites.
- Having very short pages for important services.
- Ignoring that a new website needs time and signals.

## What to prepare before starting

Before asking for a quote or starting a redesign, prepare a few basic things:

- Access to Search Console and CMS.
- List of pages that should appear in Google.
- Keywords or phrases where visibility is desired.
- Check sitemap, robots.txt and noindex.
- Minimum relevant content on each important page.

## How to know whether it works

A website should not be judged only by how it looks on launch day. Track whether people reach the important pages, understand the offer and send clear enquiries.

In Search Console, check whether pages are indexed, which queries create impressions, which pages receive clicks and which errors appear. If there are impressions without clicks, the issue may be title or description, not indexing.

## Conclusion

When a site does not appear in Google, start with concrete checks: indexing, technical access, content and structure. Only after that should you discuss rankings and long-term SEO strategy.
